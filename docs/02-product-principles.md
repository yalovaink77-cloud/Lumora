# Lumora Product Principles

## 1. Mother-first Experience

Lumora prioritizes the mother's needs, clarity, dignity, and wellbeing. Every user experience should reduce unnecessary stress, support informed decisions, and respect the mother's autonomy.

---

## 2. Child-centered Data Model

Each child is treated as an independent lifelong entity rather than an extension of a user account. Health records, milestones, memories, and development belong to the child's timeline and can be shared only through appropriate permissions.

---

## 3. Family-centered Ecosystem

Families care together. Lumora supports collaboration between parents, guardians, and caregivers while preserving individual privacy, consent, and responsibility.

---

## 4. Privacy by Design

Privacy is a core product principle. Lumora collects only the information necessary to provide value, protects sensitive data by default, and gives families clear control over their information.

---

## 5. Medical Safety First

Health-related information must always prioritize safety. Lumora never replaces professional medical advice, avoids creating false confidence, and clearly distinguishes educational information from medical guidance.

---

## 6. AI as an Assistant, Not a Doctor

AI exists to organize information, explain concepts, answer questions, and help families prepare for healthcare decisions. AI does not diagnose, prescribe treatments, or replace qualified healthcare professionals.

---

## 7. Lifelong Continuity

Lumora is designed to accompany families throughout life. Pregnancy, childhood, adolescence, and adulthood are connected through a continuous timeline rather than isolated applications or disconnected modules.

---

## 8. Documentation Before Implementation

Product, business, and architectural decisions must be documented, reviewed, and agreed upon before implementation begins. Missing context must always be clarified instead of assumed.

---

## 9. User Ownership

Families remain the owners of their own data. Lumora exists to help organize, preserve, and protect that information—not to take ownership of it.
